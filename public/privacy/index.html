Overview & Project Nature

Anti-Bot is a non-commercial, open-source hobby project built by an individual developer in their spare time. It has no business purpose, generates no revenue, and serves no commercial interest whatsoever. It exists purely because building it was fun.

This Privacy Policy explains what data is processed when you use the Anti-Bot Discord bot or its website, and how it is handled — in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, Regulation (EU) 2016/679).

🌐 Anti-Bot is fully open-source. You can audit exactly how your data is handled by reading the source code on GitHub. No secrets.

Legal Basis for Processing

In accordance with Article 6 of the GDPR, all data processing carried out by Anti-Bot relies on the following legal bases:

  • Legitimate Interest — Art. 6(1)(f)
    Processing Discord user and guild data to provide the anti-spam protection service. Server administrators have a legitimate interest in protecting their communities from automated bot accounts. This interest is not overridden by users' rights, as only the minimum necessary data is processed and no data is used for any other purpose.
  • Performance of a Service — Art. 6(1)(b)
    Processing session authentication data to allow server administrators to access and manage their bot configuration through the dashboard.

💡 Anti-Bot does not rely on consent as a legal basis. No consent pop-ups or consent management is required because the legal bases above apply. The only cookie used is strictly necessary for authentication.

How We Use Your Data

The data we collect is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Authentication — Verifying that you are a real Discord user and that you have the necessary permissions to manage the servers shown to you in the dashboard.
  • Service Delivery — Reading and writing your guild configuration so that the bot behaves according to your settings.
  • Dashboard Personalization — Displaying your username and avatar in the header while you are logged in.
  • Security — Preventing unauthorized access to guild configurations belonging to servers you do not manage.
  • Bot Operation — Detecting and acting upon messages sent in configured quarantine channels by non-whitelisted accounts.

🚫 We never use your data for marketing, profiling, advertising, analytics, machine learning training, or any purpose beyond directly operating the Anti-Bot service. This is a hobby project — there is no business motive.

Automated Decision-Making

In accordance with Article 22 of the GDPR, we inform you that Anti-Bot performs fully automated decisions that may produce significant effects on Discord server members.

Specifically, when a message is detected in a configured quarantine channel from an account that is not on the bypass list, the bot automatically applies one of the following sanctions without human review:

  • Guild Ban — Permanent removal from the server and deletion of recent message history.
  • Guild Kick — Removal from the server without a ban.
  • Timeout — Temporary communication restriction (up to 24 hours).
  • Message Deletion — Silent removal of the message, no other action.

False positives can happen. The bot acts solely on the event of a message being sent in a monitored channel — it does not analyze message content or account history. Human users who message a quarantine channel can be sanctioned. Server admins are strongly advised to configure bypass roles for all legitimate members and moderators.

Your right to human intervention: If you believe you have been incorrectly sanctioned by Anti-Bot on a server, you must contact the administrator of that Discord server directly. Anti-Bot (the project) has no ability to reverse actions taken on third-party Discord servers — only the server admin can do so. We recommend appealing through the server's designated support channel or direct message to an administrator.

Data Storage & Security

Anti-Bot is a self-hosted project. This means data is stored on the server chosen by whoever deploys the bot, not on centralized infrastructure we control. The following applies to the reference deployment:

  • Guild configurations are stored in a local database file on the host server. This file is not publicly accessible.
  • Session data is held in server-side memory only and is automatically cleared after 24 hours or upon logout. No sensitive data is stored in your browser.
  • OAuth tokens — We never store your Discord OAuth access token or refresh token beyond the immediate authentication flow.

As this is an open-source, self-hosted project, the security of any given deployment also depends on the configuration and practices of the individual who hosts it. We encourage reviewing the source code before self-hosting.

connect.sid — Session Cookie

Your Rights (GDPR)

Under the GDPR (Chapter III), you have the following rights regarding your personal data. We respect and support all of them:

  • Right of Access — Art. 15 — You can request a copy of all data we hold about you or your guild.
  • Right to Rectification — Art. 16 — You can update your guild configuration at any time through the dashboard.
  • Right to Erasure — Art. 17 — You can request deletion of your guild configuration at any time. Remove the bot from your server, or contact us. Session data is automatically deleted after 24 hours.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing — Art. 18 — You can request that we suspend processing of your data without deleting it, pending a dispute or verification.
  • Right to Data Portability — Art. 20 — We can provide your guild configuration in a machine-readable format upon request.
  • Right to Object — Art. 21 — You can object to processing based on legitimate interest at any time. The simplest exercise of this right is removing the bot from your server.
  • Rights related to automated decisions — Art. 22 — See the Automated Decision-Making section above for details on how to seek human intervention.

🏛️ Right to lodge a complaint — Art. 77: If you believe your data is being processed in violation of the GDPR, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your national supervisory authority. In France: CNIL (cnil.fr). In other EU member states, contact your local Data Protection Authority (DPA).

To exercise any right other than those you can manage directly through the dashboard, please contact us via the methods described in the Contact section. We will respond within 30 days.
